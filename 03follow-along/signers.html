Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration and one of the most influential Founding Fathers. He later became the third President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A renowned polymath, inventor, and diplomat, Franklin was a key figure in both the American Revolution and the formation of the U.S. government.
John Adams
A prominent lawyer and leader in the push for independence, Adams went on to become the second President of the U.S. He played a crucial role in advocating for the Declaration.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams, he was a fierce advocate for independence and one of the leaders of the American Revolution.
John Hancock
Known for his large, bold signature, Hancock was the president of the Continental Congress at the time and a prominent patriot in the fight for independence.
Richard Henry Lee
A Virginia delegate who proposed the resolution for independence that led to the Declaration itself.

These figures are often remembered because of their leadership roles and significant contributions to America's founding.
